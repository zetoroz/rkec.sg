Water heater replacement cost in Singapore

Hossain Md Iqbal · Project Lead, RK E&C · Updated 9 July 2026 · 8 min read

Lost your hot water, or found the heater dripping and tripping the switch? The good news is that a like-for-like swap is usually quick. In Singapore, the water heater replacement cost is typically about $250–600 to supply and install an instant heater and $350–800+ for a storage (tank) heater, with the brand and model driving most of the difference. This guide walks through storage versus instant, the signs it's time to replace, why a licensed electrical worker must do the wiring, and why it's smart to replace a tired bidet at the same time.

Storage vs instant: which water heater suits you?

There are two common types in Singapore homes, and the right one depends on your bathroom and how you use hot water.

Instant (tankless) heaters

An instant heater warms water on demand as it flows through, so there's no tank to store. It's compact, fits neatly in a small HDB bathroom, and gives endless hot water at a single outlet. The trade-off is that it draws a higher electrical current, so the circuit, cable and isolator have to suit it — this is where existing wiring often needs a check.

Storage (tank) heaters

A storage heater keeps a tank of hot water ready to go. It gives a steadier, hotter flow and copes better when two outlets run at once — handy if you like a long shower or have a rain shower plus a handspray. The tank is heavier and takes a little longer to mount, and it needs wall space above the shower.

A quick way to decide: if your bathroom is compact and only one outlet ever runs hot, an instant heater is usually the simpler, cheaper choice. If two people shower back-to-back, you run a rain shower, or you simply prefer a long hot soak without the temperature dipping, a storage tank earns its extra cost. When you are already replacing the unit, it's also worth confirming the mounting height and pipe layout — moving from one type to the other can change where the inlet and outlet sit.

Signs it's time to replace your water heater

Heaters don't last forever, and a few clear symptoms mean replacement rather than a quick fix:

  • No hot water, or never hot enough. A heating element that has failed or scaled up often can't be economically repaired on an older unit.
  • Leaking. Water dripping from the body, the safety valve or the pipe connections. A leak on an electrical appliance is not something to leave.
  • Tripping the switch. If the heater trips the RCCB or circuit breaker every time you turn it on, moisture has usually got into the element — a safety warning, not a nuisance.
  • Rust or discoloured water. Brown-tinged hot water points to a corroding tank.
  • Age and noise. Rumbling or popping from a storage tank, or a unit well past its years, is living on borrowed time.

If you ever see a leak near live wiring, switch the heater off at the isolator and have it looked at promptly.

Why a licensed electrical worker must do the wiring

You can hang the unit yourself, but the electrical connection is not a DIY job. A water heater is a high-load appliance sitting in a wet room, and Singapore's rules are clear: work on your fixed electrical installation must be carried out by a Licensed Electrical Worker under the Energy Market Authority. That covers the isolator, the earthing and the residual current protection that keeps you safe.

Getting the wiring wrong in a bathroom is a genuine shock and fire risk, and DIY electrical work can also void your home insurance. On the plumbing side, any works on your water pipes should follow PUB's requirements, and connections to the water supply are meant to be done by a licensed plumber. RK E&C carries out the swap and arranges the licensed electrical connection as part of the job, so you don't have to line up separate trades.

Safety points that matter in a wet area

  • RCCB protection. The heater circuit must sit behind a working residual current circuit breaker so power cuts the instant a fault is detected.
  • Proper earthing and isolator. A dedicated double-pole isolator switch outside the wet zone lets you cut power safely for servicing.
  • Waterproof integrity. Mountings and pipe penetrations shouldn't compromise your bathroom's waterproofing — worth pairing with good HDB bathroom waterproofing if yours is due.
  • Correct rating. An instant heater's higher current draw has to match the cable and breaker — undersized wiring overheats.

Replacing the bidet or handspray at the same time

While we're already at your bathroom with the water shut off and tools out, it's the natural moment to sort a tired bidet, handspray or its hose. A worn spray head that drips, a perished hose, or a stiff valve all get replaced quickly once we're on-site.

On its own, a bidet or handspray replacement runs about $60–150, and it usually carries its own trip charge. Bundled with the heater, you pay one visit and save that second call-out — which is exactly what the $650 job above did, pairing the new heater with a fresh bidet. It's the same logic that applies to other small fixes like leaking tap repairs in an HDB: batch them into one appointment.

If your bidet or handspray is leaking at the wall connector rather than the head, tell us when you book — sometimes it's just the T-valve or hose, and sometimes the connection point needs re-sealing. Either way it's a small job when the water is already off for the heater.

The one-visit advantage

A water heater swap, a bidet change and a quick check of the isolator and waterproofing are all bathroom jobs. Doing them together means a single trip charge, one shut-off of the water, and one tidy-up — instead of paying and waiting for separate visits. It's also easier to guarantee the result when the same team handled the whole bathroom.
